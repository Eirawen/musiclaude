# Song Contract

## Overview
- **Title**: *Matin de Boulangerie* (Bakery Morning)
- **Genre**: Pastoral chamber — impressionistic, narrative, romantic
- **Mood**: First love at 17. Innocence, warmth, the ache of a summer that will end. Southern France, flour-dusted hands, a bell above the door.
- **Duration**: ~80 measures, approximately 4-5 minutes

## Instrumentation
- **Clarinet in Bb** — the narrator. Warm, breathy, earnest. Enters tentatively, grows confident, then lingers.
- **Piano** — the world. The bakery, the village, the summer air. Establishes atmosphere, supports, then absorbs the clarinet's melody at the end.

## Structure
| Section | Measures | Tempo | Description |
|---------|----------|-------|-------------|
| I. Morning | 1–16 | ♩.=72, 6/8 | Piano alone. Simple, pastoral F major. A gentle ostinato like the rhythm of kneading dough — repetitive but warm. Sparse, pp to mp. Establishes the daily routine. |
| II. The Door Opens | 17–28 | same | Clarinet enters mp, tentative. A simple ascending phrase — curiosity, first glance. Piano harmony shifts subtly (add9 chords, suspended notes). The world tilts slightly. |
| III. Conversation | 29–48 | slightly pressing, ♩.=76 | Call and response between clarinet and piano. Phrases get longer, braver. Brief modulation to D minor (m.37) — a moment of shyness, vulnerability — then resolving warmly back. Dynamics grow to mf. |
| IV. Walking Home | 49–64 | ♩.=80, broadening | The emotional peak. Both instruments singing together in parallel and contrary motion. Modulate to Ab major (m.49) — the world opening up. Rich hairpins, mf to f. The melody blooms — wider intervals, higher register. The swell in your chest. |
| V. Alone Again | 65–80 | ♩.=69, ritardando final bars | Piano solo return. Same opening ostinato but now with richer harmony — added 7ths, a touch of chromaticism. At m.72 the clarinet's melody from Section II appears in the piano right hand — you're humming her melody without realizing it. pp, tender, ends on an unresolved add9 chord. First love doesn't resolve neatly. |

## Harmonic Plan
- **Key**: F major (the pastoral key — Beethoven's 6th)
- **Chord palette**: Major triads, add9 chords, sus4→resolution, dominant 7ths, occasional minor borrowed chords (iv, ii°). Ab major triads in the peak section.
- **Modulations**:
  - I–II: F major throughout
  - III: Brief D minor excursion (m.37–40), resolving back to F
  - IV: Modulate to Ab major (bIII — a warm, unexpected lift)
  - V: Return to F major, enriched
- **Cadence style**: Mostly half cadences and deceptive cadences — avoid strong perfect authentic cadences until the very end (and even then, leave it unresolved with an add9)

## Melodic Guidelines
- **Clarinet range**: C4 to A5 (comfortable middle-to-upper register, nothing straining)
- **Character**: Mostly stepwise with occasional expressive leaps (4ths, 6ths) at emotional moments. The main motif should be singable — something you'd catch yourself humming.
- **Motif ideas**:
  - Primary motif (clarinet, Section II): ascending stepwise phrase, 4-5 notes, ending on a gentle suspension. The "first glance" melody.
  - Piano ostinato (Section I): lilting 6/8 arpeggiation, root-fifth-octave pattern with subtle inner voice movement.
  - The primary motif transfers to piano RH in Section V — the memory.

## Rhythmic Framework
- **Tempo**: ♩.=72 (dotted quarter = 72), gently increasing to 80 at the peak, settling to 69 at the close
- **Time signature**: 6/8 throughout — the pastoral lilt, like walking through a village, the sway of a summer afternoon
- **Rhythmic feel**: Lilting, unhurried. Eighth-note pulse in the piano, longer note values in the clarinet (dotted quarters, half-bar phrases). The clarinet should feel like it floats above the piano's gentle current.

## Dynamics & Expression
- **Overall dynamic arc**: pp → mp → mf → f (peak) → pp (coda)
- **Section I**: pp–mp. Dolce, legato. Piano una corda feeling.
- **Section II**: mp. Clarinet enters espressivo. A breath mark before the first note.
- **Section III**: mp–mf. Poco a poco crescendo through the conversation. Diminuendo into the D minor moment.
- **Section IV**: mf–f. Grand crescendo with hairpins on both instruments. The biggest dynamic range. Broadening.
- **Section V**: pp. Morendo. The final bars should fade like a memory.
- **Articulations**: Primarily legato throughout. Light staccato only in piano ostinato bass notes. Clarinet: tenuto on expressive long notes, gentle accents on melodic peaks.
- **Special markings**: "dolce" at opening, "espressivo" at clarinet entry, "con moto" at conversation, "largamente" at peak, "come un ricordo" (like a memory) at coda.

## Quality Targets
- Target chord vocabulary: >= 25 unique chord types
- Target melodic range: 20+ semitones (clarinet)
- Extended chords: yes, 40%+ (add9s, 7ths, sus4s are core to the harmonic language)
- Dynamics markings: required — minimum 15 explicit markings
- Hairpin markings: required — minimum 8 crescendo/decrescendo wedges
- Articulation markings: required — legato phrases, tenuto, occasional staccato
- Expression markings: required — tempo text, espressivo, dolce, morendo
- Minimum rhythmic variety: 6+ unique durations

## Additional Notes
- This piece is being composed for a blind listening experiment (007). The same contract will be given to 4 separate composition sessions, each using a different feature profile for feedback. The listener will not know which profile produced which version.
- The emotional authenticity matters more than technical complexity. This should feel *felt*, not constructed.
- The unresolved ending is intentional and important — do not add a perfect cadence at the end. The add9 or sus4 final chord should hang in the air.
- Italian/French expression markings preferred over English.
